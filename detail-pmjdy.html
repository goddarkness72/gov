Overview

Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) is a national mission for financial inclusion to ensure access to financial services, namely banking, credit, insurance and pension. Launched by Prime Minister on August 28, 2014, this scheme aims to provide bank accounts to all households to promote financial inclusion.

Objective: To bring every household in India into the formal financial system by providing access to basic banking services.

Benefits

  • Universal access to banking services
  • Direct benefit transfer to poor households
  • Financial security through insurance coverage
  • Pension schemes availability
  • Promotes digital financial inclusion
  • Entrepreneurship opportunities through credit access

Eligibility

Who can open account:

  • Any individual (aged 18+) with valid KYC documents
  • For children: Parent or guardian can open account
  • No income criteria or educational qualification required

How to Open Account

Step Process
1 Visit your nearest bank branch
2 Fill PMJDY account opening form
3 Submit KYC documents (Aadhar, PAN, Voter ID, etc.)
4 Complete biometric verification
5 Get account created and receive debit card

Financial Inclusion Impact

Since launch in August 2014, PMJDY has:

  • Opened over 46+ crore (460 million) bank accounts
  • Provided debit cards to eligible account holders
  • Disbursed benefits directly to beneficiaries
  • Integrated with Direct Benefit Transfer schemes
  • Promoted financial literacy and digital payments

Related Schemes

PMJDY works in conjunction with:

  • Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT): Transfer subsidies and benefits directly to accounts
  • Atal Pension Yojana (APY): Provides pension for unorganized workers
  • Pradhan Mantri Risk Protection Yojana: Insurance schemes
